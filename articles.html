What is Fake News?

Fake news consists of news, stories, or hoaxes intentionally created to mislead or deceive readers. These fabricated stores can:

- Influence public opinion.

- Push a political agenda.

- Cause confusion.

- Generate profit for online publishers.

Fake news can take two primary forms:

1. Comedic Fake News - Satirical websites explicitly state their intent to entertain in their "About Us" pages.

2. Deceptive Fake News - Sites masquerade as legitimate news sources, discreetly blending falsehoods with actual information to mislead users.

Impacts of Fake News

Fake news affect individuals and society in several ways:

Confusion - Misinformation spreads false information that discourages or misguides individuals.

Example: False fliers distributed at Bates College discouraged students from voting by misleading them about voter registration requirements.Due to this, some students may have chosen not to vote due to this misinformation, showing how fake news can influence actions.

Propaganda - Fake news plays a major role in politics, manipulating opinions to tarnish reputations or shoft public support.

Example: A fabrifacted report claimed Barack Obama and Hillary Clinton promised amnesty yo undocumented immigrants who voted Democrats. Neither actually made such promises. This misinformation was decigned to damage reputations and manipulate voters.

How to Identify Fake News

To spot misinformation, consider the following checks:

1. Source Reliability - Verify that the website belongs to a reputable organization and check if its articles appear on official platforms.

2. Clickbait Headlines - Fake news often uses sensationalist or shocking headlines in ALL CAPS with exclamation points.

3. Cross-Checking Reports - See if legitimate news sources (e.g., GMA News, ABS-CBN) also report the story.

4. Fact & Evidence Verification - Fake news may contain incorrect timelines or statistics, so always trace the original data.
